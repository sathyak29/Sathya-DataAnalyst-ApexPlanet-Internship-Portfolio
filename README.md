## 📊 Dataset Overview

- **Name:** Superstore Sales Dataset
- **Source:** Provided by ApexPlanet during the internship program
- **Format:** Excel (.xls)
- **Size:** 9,994 rows × 21 columns
- **Domain:** Retail Sales & Business Analytics
- **Description:** The dataset covers US-based retail sales transactions including customer details, order information, product categories, shipping modes, and financial metrics like sales, profit, quantity, and discount.

### 📋 Columns at a Glance

| Category | Columns |
|----------|---------|
| **Order Info** | Row ID, Order ID, Order Date, Ship Date, Ship Mode |
| **Customer Info** | Customer ID, Customer Name, Segment |
| **Location** | Country, City, State, Postal Code, Region |
| **Product Info** | Product ID, Category, Sub-Category, Product Name |
| **Financials** | Sales, Quantity, Discount, Profit |

- ✅ **No missing values** across all 21 columns
- 📅 **Date Range:** 2016 onwards
- 🌍 **Coverage:** United States (multiple regions)

## 🔍 Key Findings Across All Tasks

### 🧹 Task 1 — Data Immersion & Wrangling
- Dataset had **9,994 rows × 21 columns** with **zero missing values and zero duplicates**
- Fixed data types, standardized text fields, and grouped regions into **Coastal & Central**
- Engineered new features: **Order Year** and **Order Month** from Order Date

### 📊 Task 2 — EDA & Business Intelligence
- **Phones** ($330K) & **Chairs** ($328K) were top revenue products;
- **Technology** led profit by category ($145K);
- **West** region had highest sales ($725K)
- **Consumer segment** generated the most revenue ($1.16M) & profit ($134K);
- **6 states ran at a loss** — Texas (-$25.7K) being the worst
- High discounts (>40%) averaged **-$107 profit per order** vs $67 with no discount — proven via SQL & correlation analysis (r = -0.22)

### 📈 Task 3 — Deep Dive Analysis & Interactive Dashboard
- **Technology** had the highest profit margin (~17%);
-  **Furniture** the lowest (~2.5%) with Tables & Bookcases generating losses
- Discounts above **30%** consistently resulted in negative profit;
- Furniture received the highest avg. discounts (25–30%)
- Built a **3-page interactive Power BI dashboard** covering KPIs, deep-dive analysis, and monthly trends with 6-month forecast

### 📉 Task 4 — Data Storytelling & Statistical Validation
- Sales grew **51% from 2014–2017** but profit growth lagged — discounts above **20% consistently cause losses**
- Statistical tests confirmed key findings: **T-Test** (discount impact), **Chi-Square** (category vs profit), and **T-Test** (Technology vs Furniture) — all significant
- Recommended capping discounts at 20%, focusing on **Technology** (highest ROI), and improving **Central region** & **Tables** sub-category pricing
